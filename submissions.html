Submissions Guidelines

Contact the IMR Manager directly to initiate your project's consultation, then download our Excel form:

Sample Submission Form (ver.10-Jan.2019)

This form is to be filled out with all of your details for tracking purposes and has sample preparation and shipping guidelines (please pay careful attention for international clients). You will be given a unique project/sample identifier in the form of "IMR-YYYYMM-00" which will allow sample tracking, will show up on your billing information and be listed in the online queue.

In order to offer as flexible (and economical) a service as possible to the community, we will not be insisting that clients fulfill complete multiplexed runs of samples. The main way sequencing is done is that your samples are mixed with other clients - this may take a bit longer (primarily for NextSeq/PacBio runs - MiSeq runs are always full) in the pipeline as we wait for the remainder of the run slots to be filled by other projects/individuals. Dalhousie clients (since internal) and Commercial clients (since they help subsidize for academics by paying a premium) receive queue priority, but for external academics, in extreme cases of important deadlines, there are some options for expediting your service (contact us).

Sample Minimum Requirements

  For amplicon sequencing (MiSeq):
  • Concentration: >1 ng/µL (preferrably >10 ng/µL); we do not re-quantify incoming DNAs for amplicon PCRs
  • Volume: 10 µL of each sample (this volume is enough for two different targets, such as 16S + 18S; if selecting more targets, send 15 uL or more)
  • Quality: "clean" DNA coming from commercial kit or proven manual method; absolutely clean spec ratios not required as PCR is relatively robust, but inhibitors must be removed
  For client-prepared pool of amplicons (MiSeq):
  • Purified final library at >10 ng/µL in at least 10 µL; we re-quantify incoming libraries (not oligos below) using Qubit
  • If using custom primers, provide 10 µL each of Read1 + Read2 + Index primers at 100 µM
  For genome sequencing (MiSeq/NextSeq):
  • Concentration: >0.2 ng/µL (preferrably >1 ng/µL); we re-quantify incoming DNAs using Qubit or Quant-iT
  • Volume: 10 µL of each sample
  • Quality: spec A260/280 and A260/230 ratios as close to 1.8 and 2.0 as possible (or over), since library prep (Nextera Flex) is sensitive to DNA quality
  For genome sequencing (PacBio):
  • Amount: >1.5 µg (at least 10 ng/µL) of high-molecular weight; we re-quantify incoming DNAs using Qubit or Quant-iT
  • Volume: max. 150 µL of each sample
  • Quality: spec A260/280 and A260/230 ratios as close to 1.8 and 2.0 as possible (or over), since library prep (PacBio) is very sensitive to DNA quality
  For metagenomic sequencing (MiSeq/NextSeq):
  • Concentration: >0.2 ng/µL (preferrably >1 ng/µL); we re-quantify incoming DNAs using Qubit or Quant-iT
  • Volume: 10 µL of each sample
  • Quality: spec A260/280 and A260/230 ratios as close to 1.8 and 2.0 as possible (or over), since library prep (Nextera Flex) is sensitive to DNA quality
